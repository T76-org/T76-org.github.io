Important warnings and safety precautions

Dr. PD is designed to help you explore the world of USB-PD in a safe and controlled way, but it's important to remember that USB-PD devices can deliver high levels of power, and that there is always a risk of damage to your devices or to Dr. PD itself if you are not careful when testing.

Therefore, we strongly recommend that you always take appropriate safety precautions when testing USB-PD devices with Dr. PD, such as using high-quality cables and connectors, making sure that your devices are on a non-flammable surface, and keeping a fire extinguisher nearby just in case. We also recommend not leaving Dr. PD unattended while testing, especially when testing at high power levels or with devices that have unknown or poorly implemented protection mechanisms.

In addition to these general safety precautions, there are some specific warnings and precautions that you should keep in mind when using Dr. PD:

  • Always make sure to set appropriate overvoltage and overcurrent protection thresholds in Dr. PD before testing, and to reset the protections if they are triggered during testing. This will help prevent damage to your devices and to Dr. PD itself in case of abnormal conditions.
  • When testing EPR sources at high power levels, make sure to use appropriate safety precautions, such as using a high-quality USB-C cable that is rated for EPR, making sure that your source and Dr. PD are on a non-flammable surface, and keeping a fire extinguisher nearby just in case.
  • When testing a source device in Sink mode, make sure that you don't have any devices connected to Port 2. Dr. PD includes a lock-out feature that disconnects Port 2 from the USB-PD bus and VBUS line in order to protect any device that you might have inadvertently left connected to that port, but it's still a good idea to make sure that you don't have any devices connected to Port 2 while testing a source device on Port 1 in Sink mode.
  • Do not, under any circumstance, exceed the voltage and current limits of Dr. PD (60V and 6A) when testing USB-PD devices. Dr. PD is designed to handle these levels safely, but exceeding them can cause serious damage to the device.
  • When testing a source device in Sink mode, make sure to use an appropriate load that is within the specifications of the source device and of Dr. PD. Using a load that draws too much current can cause damage to the source device, to Dr. PD, or to both.
  • When using they sync output in pull-down mode, do not exceed 3.3V and 10mA on the sync output. This is the maximum voltage and current that the sync output can handle safely, and exceeding these limits can cause damage to Dr. PD or to any devices connected to the sync output.

By following these warnings and precautions, you can help ensure that your testing with Dr. PD is safe and successful, and that you don't accidentally damage your devices or Dr. PD itself while exploring the world of USB-PD.
